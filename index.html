UX Research

on

Metro Online Grocery Service

Overview

It was mid June 2020. Canada was in it’s third month of lockdown due to the Covid-19 pandemic. I was a student then and as a part of my class project, I conducted a UX research on Metro Inc which is the third largest grocer in Canada. I was particularly interested for the topic, as during the lockdown, I noticed a significant change in the grocery shopping behaviour among my friends, family and specifically in myself. I shifted to eating at home, limited the frequency of store visits, became more dependent on online purchase. So I was curious to know the readiness of a top Canadian grocer to serve its online customer base.

Research Objective

The objective is to evaluate the user experience of Metro online grocery service based on the preexisting and recent needs emerged from covid-19 pandemic.

Key research questions and areas:

  • 1. What are the online grocery shopping behaviour of customers during Covid-19 pandemic?
  • 2. What are the areas of improvement for metro to meet customer’s needs?
  • 3. How convenient it is to use Metro Online Grocery Service? What are the key usability and accessibility issues?

Duration

June 3- June 17, 2020

Research Insights

1. The Common Patterns

During the research synthesis, I used "Affinity Mapping" technique to find out the common patterns of behaviour among the participants. From these patterns, I identified area of opportunities for online grocery businesses that will help them to retain/get more customers by improving user experiences.

2. The Unique Needs

During user interview, I identified some unique needs of users which was further clarified after the usability test.

Users with special dietary needs wants to see the detail nutrition facts of the product in the online platform of grocery service.

“I have diabetic and my wife is currently maintaining Keto diet. Both of us needs to be extra cautious about what we eat in a day. Before pandemic, we used to check the detail nutrition facts of the product which I really miss now. As most of the app only have product image but not the product details, especially the nutrition facts.” -----Quote from user's interview

Area of Improvement for Metro

Currently, both the metro app and website contains only an image and description of the product but not the details nutritional facts. Users who have specific dietary needs may find it inconvenient to order from online.

A sample detail nutrition facts which can be included to improve user experience.

Users who are price conscious expect the in-store promotional offer available in all platform.

“Few days back I saw a great deal on salmon and steak in Metro. I wanted to order the items through Cornershop as I already have it's yearly subscription. But I found that Metro’s deal is not available if I order through Cornershop. I thought to directly order from Metro. But since I have already paid $100 for Cornershop's yearly subscription, I didn’t want to spend extra." -----Quote from user's interview

Area of Improvement for Metro

Besides website/ app, Metro’s grocery is available to its users through other applications i.e. Cornershop. It may better serve the price conscious customers, if its promotional offers are available in all platform.

Users who are frequent buyer and have limited storage at their home needs affordable payment plan in terms of minimum purchase order/ delivery fee.

"I live in a small apartment. As there is not enough storage, I can't order in bulk. Also, I usually don’t plan much for grocery. So my preference is placing frequent order, but in small quantity. Few days back, I ordered few items online worth $20. But the minimum purchase amount was $ 30. So I ended up ordering things that I didn’t need" -----Quote from user's interview

Area of Improvement for Metro

To avail Metro’s online service, the minimum purchase order is $50 before tax and fees. The delivery fee is additional $11.99. During user interview, participants mentioned the amount is too expensive for them.

3. The Usability Issues of Mobile App

Difficulty to Search for Products

I asked 2 participants to perform a specific task in metro mobile app - “add 10 items of their choice in the cart and proceed till checkout”. Both the participants had difficulty navigating to search for products. After opening the app, they spent approximately 3 minutes on average just to find out that there is a “Shop online on metro.ca” link which they needs to click to start searching for products. Participants mentioned that they expected a search bar for products immediately after opening the the app.

4. Heuristics Analysis

Error Prevention

During sign up process of a user, metro does not authenticate the user account. As a result, user can create an account in metro app/website with an email address even if he/she does not belong to it.I created an account using a random email address “noone@gmail.com” in metro website for testing purpose. The site allowed me to create the account without authenticating it with the associated email address. (Picture for Reference)

User can create an account in Metro even if they dont belong to it

Help users recognize, diagnose, and recover from errors

Metro website does not allow a user to create an account with password that has special character (i.e. @, $) in it and shows an error message saying “Password in invalid. It must contain at least 8 characters, one of which is a number”; This can create confusion as the message does not inform the user that the password cannot contain special characters in it.

The error message displayed when the password does not matches with the requirement during account creation.

5. The Accessibility Issues

Absence of focus indicator

When user tabs through the content of the website, there is no focus indicator for some item of the page. This might be difficult for users who cannot use mouse and dependent on keyboard, as he/she will have no idea which UI item he/she currently in.

Focus does not follow logical order

When a screen reader user adds an item in the cart, the focus skips the option to increase(+) / decrease(-) the item. As a result, they cannot immediately change the number of item chosen in the cart. The only option available at this stage is to remove the item from the cart. To increase/decrease the number of item, the user needs to tab through the entire page and come again to increase(+)/ decrease(-) button. Only then, the increase(+)/ decrease(-) receives the focus which is inconvenient for the users.

Absence of alt text

There are many images in the website that does not have alt text such as the logo in the home page.

Color contrast

Element has insufficient color contrast of 2.99 (foreground color: #959595, background color: #ffffff, font size: 10.5pt (14px), font weight: normal). Expected contrast ratio of 4.5:1

Absence of semantic element

The website does not use semantic element which will create difficulties for a screen reader to navigate the site. Example includes: There is no "main" html element in the homepage; Homepage does not have level 1 heading;

Reflections on My Work

As a designer and a user, I would rate Metro's digital experience as 2 out of 5.

Few usability issues where I would like to bring immediate attention to are:

  • "Search for items" is one of the most common use cases that users perform in a grocery app. But, in Metro app, it was the most difficult task to do and induced negative emotion.
  • Online grocery shopping is new experience for many people. During the user research, I noticed users feels anxious providing their credit card information in these platforms. In this scenario, the error prevention issue that I found during heuristics evaluation (creating user account without authenticating) would create trust issues.
